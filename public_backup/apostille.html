What is an Apostille?

An apostille is a special certification that verifies the authenticity of a document for use in countries that are members of the Hague Apostille Convention. It confirms the authority of the notary or official who signed your document.

Hague Convention Countries

We process apostilles for use in over 120 member countries including France, Germany, China, and Brazil.

Non-Hague Countries

For countries not in the Hague Convention (like UAE or Egypt), we provide full authentication through the State Department and foreign embassies.

Getting Started

To begin the apostille process:

  1. Contact us with details about your document and destination country
  2. Email or bring us the original document (or certified copy)
  3. We'll advise on any additional requirements (translation, copies, etc.)
  4. Choose your processing speed and payment method
  5. We handle all certifications and return your document ready for international use

Important: Some documents (like birth certificates) must be certified copies from the issuing agency - we cannot notarize original vital records.
