Remote Online Notarization Services

At Vantage Notary, we offer Remote Online Notarization (RON) services that allow you to have your documents notarized from anywhere with an internet connection. Our remote notaries are authorized by the State of Indiana to perform online notarizations using secure video technology, providing convenience without sacrificing security or legal validity.

What is Remote Online Notarization?

Remote Online Notarization (RON) is a process that allows a notary public to notarize documents for signers who are not physically present. Instead, the signer and notary connect via secure audio-visual technology. The notary verifies the signer's identity through knowledge-based authentication and ID verification, witnesses the electronic signing of documents, and applies an electronic notary seal and signature.

The Remote Notarization Process

Our remote notarization process is straightforward and secure:

  1. Schedule an appointment - Contact us to arrange a convenient time for your remote notarization session.
  2. Document preparation - Upload your documents to our secure platform prior to your appointment.
  3. Identity verification - Complete knowledge-based authentication questions and present your government-issued ID via video.
  4. Video session - Connect with our notary via secure video conference at your scheduled time.
  5. Electronic signing - Sign your documents electronically while the notary witnesses via video.
  6. Notarization completion - The notary applies their electronic seal and signature to complete the notarization.
  7. Document delivery - Receive your notarized documents electronically, with the option for paper copies if needed.

Documents Eligible for Remote Notarization

Most documents that can be notarized in person can also be notarized remotely, including:

  • Powers of Attorney
  • Affidavits
  • Sworn Statements
  • Real Estate Documents
  • Business Agreements
  • Loan Documents
  • Medical Directives
  • Consent Forms

Note: Some documents may not be eligible for remote notarization due to specific state laws or requirements. Please contact us if you have questions about a specific document type.

Technical Requirements

To participate in a remote notarization session, you will need:

  • A computer, tablet, or smartphone with a camera and microphone
  • A stable internet connection
  • A valid, government-issued photo ID
  • An email address to receive instructions and documents
